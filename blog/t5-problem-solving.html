Q1. Tell your non-tech friend a story about a time you got blocked on a simple problem and when you solved Solved a problem in an elegant way

The first time I got block on a simple problem I was adding another block of code to a function. I had gone on and updated the rest of the code as I was in a rush and wanted to update the other sections. Big mistake. I went through and updated a later development of my code - which was working fine by the way - however at the time I was getting steadily more and more frustrated trying to address a problem within code that looked completely fine to me. The more frustrated I got, the more anxious I got and the more anxious I got the less effectively I could solve the problem. I actually ended up stopping and going to bed. I slept on it and thought about it away from the the computer for a little while. I came back and step through the exercise and immediately saw the minor error I had made -that I had been in too much of a rush to address at the time had one misnamed variable that meant that my entire program was not working. I learned that I needed to slow down and go through the process properly. I know that I need to give myself space and to take off the pressure in order to solve the problem.

As for solving a problem in an elegant way - I’m not sure that I have yet. I think if anything I have been going through these exercises and looking at how I can use the limited knowledge of code that I have to deliver what I need to in the most easy, chic and effective way. I think this is a combination of not being super over the material and utilising my design background. Ask me this question in a couple of months.
